About Carlos Alvarado

My name is Carlos Alvarado. I am from Chelsea, Massachusetts and have lived there all my life. My family immigrated there from Honduras in the 80s and 90s. Their immigrant stories along with many others in Chelsea have molded me into the man that I am today. While my parents placed heavy emphasis on education when I was a child, it took me some time to figure that out for myself. I always loved to learn but never realized how much of a privilege it is to be able to learn. I attended Beacon Academy, a school for underprivileged, innercity kids in Boston that prepares them for private high schools and all that entails attending those types of school. We gained both academic and social education. Classes ranged from Algebra to Latin to "Life at a PWI" to name a few. After leaving this school, I attended Milton Academy, a boarding school where I spent 4 years. Being at this school, I was able to play varsity sports and enjoyed my time on the soccer team as it built my sense of camaraderie. Being away from home made my heart grow fonder for Chelsea while also realizing there should be more kids like me experiencing the life I had and I wanted to find a way to tangibly achieve this. That is when I discovered Babson College.

As I finished my time in high school, I hoped to find purpose in my actions, not wanting to waste time as the pandemic came to a close and the world began opening up again. It wasn't until the end of my senior year in High School when I realized where my passion was. After rekindling my friendship with a childhood friend, I began working with him to grow the clothing brand he was working, a brand named Kliq. After seeing the backend of what goes into everything; from inception of an idea to the rollout and everything inbetween, I fell in love. While normally, I would have to choose between my passion and education, coming to Babson allowed me to intertwine the two and allowed me to develop the brand further than I would have been able to in any other circumstance. My time here at Babson has been molded around both developing my skills pertaining to strategy, consulting, and project management while also pursuing experiences that would be applicable to Kliq and my role as the COO of the brand. Beyond the courses and experiences I have had here at Babson, the most important and influential part would be the relationships I have built with my peers. Following my freshman year, I felt I had not lived to the fullest which prompted me to join Alpha Kappa Psi, the oldest and largest business fraternity in the world. Being drawn in my that moniker, I hesitantly joined and did not regret it. I was exposed to the most down to earth and genuine people I had met at Babson and completely shattered my perception of what someone needed to be like to succeed. Joining AKPsi allowed me to build my confidence even greater while understanding the importance of paying everything you are given forward. I was taken under the wing of senior brothers who inspired me to continue the cycle and serve as a mentor to younger members of the fraternity now that I am the senior. Overall, my experience at Babson has allowed me to cut out the unnecessary of my life while also reinforcing the type of impact and man I want to be moving forward. My time here allowed me to understand what my high school aspirations can tangibly look like and motivated me to continue my pursuit of not only academic educational equality but democratization of social capital as well.

My Interests

Growing up, I was extremely passionate about sports as my parents had me try everything at least once before I decided it wasn't for me. I played football, baseball, basketball, and soccer to name a few. After years of experience and learning about each sport, I realized that my favorite was soccer. I have been a Real Madrid supporter all my life and used to dream of playing for the club as a child. After realizing that I would not be the next Cristiano Ronaldo, I pivoted to my current passion for fashion. (unintended rhyme!) This passion was sparked by my aforementioned involvement in Kliq with my best friend Javier. While I had always loved to dress myself and been intrigued by sneakers and clothes, joining Kliq allowed for me to learn first-hand about everything that intrigued me. I love the logistics that go into planning a collection and because of this, I have fallen in love with studying the rollouts of other businesses and brands. I have romanticized the process in a way that my hobby has become my dream and my passion. I have become a student of the industry, hoping to be a sponge whenever possible.
